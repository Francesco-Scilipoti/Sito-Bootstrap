NFL CAPS

The National Football League, also identified by the acronym NFL, is the world's largest professional American football league, consisting of 32 franchises from 30 cities in the United States. In fact, the two largest U.S. metropolises are home to two franchises: the Giants and Jets in New York, and the Rams and Chargers in Los Angeles.

In 2012 it was the professional club sports tournament with the highest average attendance per match: 67,591 spectators per game compared with 45,116 in the Fußball-Bundesliga, the German soccer league, and 34,602 in the English Premier League.

NBA CAPS

The National Basketball Association, commonly known as the NBA is a professional basketball league in North America. The league consists of 30 teams (including 29 in the United States and 1 in Canada) and is one of the major professional sports leagues in the United States and Canada. It is the top men's professional basketball league in the world.

It was founded in New York on June 6, 1946 as the Basketball Association of America (BAA). On August 3, 1949, the league adopted the name National Basketball Association following its merger with the rival National Basketball League (NBL). In 1976, the NBA and the American Basketball Association (ABA) merged, adding four franchises to the NBA. The NBA regular season runs from October to April, with each team playing 82 games. The league's playoff tournament extends through June. As of 2020, NBA players are the highest paid athletes in the world by average annual salary per player.

NHL CAPS

The NHL (National Hockey League) is the most prestigious professional ice hockey league in the world, consisting of 32 teams from the United States and Canada. In Canada, especially in Quebec, it is also known as Ligue nationale de field hockey (LNH).

The NHL is the world's premier ice hockey league, bringing together the best teams from the United States and Canada. Teams are divided into two conferences, and each team plays 82 games during the regular season. The best 16 teams advance to the playoffs, a knockout tournament that culminates in the Stanley Cup, the most prestigious trophy in ice hockey.

The top soccer leagues in Europe, generally considered, are the English Premier League, Spanish La Liga, German Bundesliga, Italian Serie A, and French Ligue 1. These leagues are famous for their level of competition, great teams, world-class players, and high level of economic investment.
